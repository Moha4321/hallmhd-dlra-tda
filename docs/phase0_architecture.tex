\subsection{Memory formula}

The state tensor in Tucker format (rank $R$) occupies:
\[
  M_\text{state}^\text{DLRA}
  = \bigl[(N_x + N_y + N_z)R + R^3\bigr] \cdot N_f \cdot 8\,\text{B}.
\]
Peak usage with 5 ETDRK4 buffers and tSVD workspace:
\[
  M_\text{peak}^\text{DLRA} = 6 \times M_\text{state}^\text{DLRA}.
\]
Full-rank peak (5 copies + FFT workspace $\approx 1.2\times$):
\[
  M_\text{peak}^\text{full} = 5 \times 1.2 \times N_x N_y N_z \cdot N_f \cdot 8\,\text{B}.
\]

\subsection{Note on proposal Table~5 discrepancy}
\label{sec:d3-note}

The proposal (\S8.2) states $128\times128\times64 \times 7 \times 8\,\text{B}
\approx 3.7\,\text{GB}$.  The correct calculation gives
$128\times128\times64\times7\times8 = 58{,}720{,}256\,\text{B} \approx 0.055\,\text{GB}$
(binary) / $0.059\,\text{GB}$ (SI).  The proposal figure is approximately $63\times$ too
large, likely resulting from a unit conversion error in the written document.
The code in \texttt{parameters.py} is arithmetically correct.
The RAM constraint is therefore non-binding at all target grid sizes
below $512\times512\times128$.  This is a favourable finding: memory is
not the bottleneck for Phase~1.

\noindent\textbf{Source file:} \texttt{src/hallmhd/parameters.py},
\texttt{src/hallmhd/memory\_budget.py}

\section{Decision D4 --- Hardware Profiling}

% =============================================================================

\begin{sealbox}{D4: Profiling results --- SEALED}
All profiling ran on Apple Mac Mini (M1, 8\,GB unified RAM) under
\texttt{JAX\_PLATFORMS=cpu} (Metal backend unavailable due to
\texttt{jax-metal} 0.10.0 API incompatibility).
CPU float64 timings are the conservative Hero Run planning baseline
as specified in the proposal (\S8.3).
M4 16\,GB Metal timings to be recorded as supplementary data when
available; they will be faster and do not affect the go/no-go decisions.
\end{sealbox}

\subsection{D4a --- 3D real FFT throughput}

Platform: Apple M1, NumPy 2.3.5, Apple Accelerate BLAS, float64.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrrrr}
\toprule
Resolution & FFT mean (ms) & FFT std (ms) &
  ETDRK4 step (ms) & Hero Run (days) \\
\midrule
$64\times64\times32$    &  1.53 & 0.22 &  22.9 & 0.04 \\
$128\times128\times64$  & 15.73 & 7.06 & 236.0 & 0.41 \\
$256\times256\times128$ & 130.03 & 2.46 & 1950.4 & 3.39 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent
Proposal ceiling: Hero Run $\leq 5$ days.
All three resolutions are within ceiling.
The $128\times128\times64$ baseline ETDRK4 step is $\approx 236\,\text{ms}$
on CPU; the JAX-JIT CPU path gives $\approx 33\,\text{ms}$ (float32 due to
JAX x64 not enabled).

\paragraph{JAX x64 warning.}
JAX defaulted to float32 despite requesting float64 (\texttt{jax.numpy.ones}
with \texttt{dtype=float64} silently truncated).
\textbf{Action required before Phase~1:} add
\texttt{jax.config.update("jax\_enable\_x64", True)} at the top of all
simulation entry points, or set \texttt{JAX\_ENABLE\_X64=1} in the
environment.  This is critical --- the solver \emph{must} run in float64.
